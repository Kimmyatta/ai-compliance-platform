A Covered Entity may determine that health information is not individually identifiable health information only if: a. A person with appropriate knowledge and experience, applying generally accepted statistical and scientific principles and methods, determines the risk is very small that the information could be used alone or in combination with reasonably available information by a recipient to identify the individual who is the subject of the information; and that person documents the methods and results of the analysis that justify such determination; or b. The following identifiers of the individual or of relatives, employers or household members of the individual, are removed: vi. Names; vii. All geographic subdivisions smaller than a State, except for the initial three digits of a zip code in certain circumstances; viii. All elements of dates except year and all ages over 89; ix. Telephone numbers; x. Fax numbers; xi. Electronic mail addresses; xii. Social security numbers; xiii.
