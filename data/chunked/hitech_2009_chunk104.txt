Beginning in 2011, eligible physicians will receive up to $15,000 in the first payment year, $12,000 in the second year, $8,000 in the third year, $4,000 in the fourth year, and $2,000 in the fifth, and final, year. Early EHR adopters whose first payment year is 2011 or 2012 will receive up to $18,000 (instead of $15,000) for that year. Eligible physicians first becoming meaningful EHR users after 2013 will receive fewer payments, and those who do not adopt EHRs until after 2014 will receive no bonus. For eligible physicians practicing in health professional shortage areas, the incentive payment amounts are increased by 10%. No incentive payments will be made after 2016. Incentive payments are not available for hospital-based physicians. Eligible physicians who are not meaningful users of certified HIT systems by 2015 will see their Medicare payments reduced by the following amounts: 1% in 2015, 2% in 2016, 3% in 2017 and in each subsequent year.
