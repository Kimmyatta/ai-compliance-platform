General B. Wrongful Disclosure 7 HIPAA: THE HEALTH INSURANCE PORTABILITY AND ACCOUNTABILITY ACT OF 1996 I. What is HIPAA? HIPAA is a federal law passed by Congress in 1996 for the purpose of improving the portability and continuity of health insurance coverage, to combat waste, fraud and abuse in health insurance and health care delivery, to simplify the administration of health insurance, and for other purposes.
