The Secretary also must create an HIT Research Center to serve as a forum for exchanging knowledge and experience, providing technical assistance to health information networks, and learning about using HIT in medically underserved communities. Finally, the Secretary must fund the creation and operation of HIT Regional Extension Centers, affiliated with nonprofit organizations, to provide assistance to providers in the region. Priority will be given to assisting public, nonprofit, and critical access hospitals, community health centers, individual and small group practices, and entities that serve the uninsured, underinsured, and medically underserved individuals. Regional centers are permitted to receive up to four years of funding, to cover up to 50% of their capital and annual operating and maintenance expenditures. Each center that receives financial support must be evaluated biennially.
